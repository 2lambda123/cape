4The :mod:`cape.attdb` module is named for its original intention of 

5handling "databases" for the Aero Task Team of the Space Launch System 

6program. The databases created for technical disciplines tend to differ 

7significantly and fundamentally from the typical databases in the 

8software world, which are little more than a series of spreadsheets in 

9a different data format. 

10 

11Technical databases also contain "lookup" rules and have meaning at 

12conditions that are not directly represented in the database. For 

13example, an integrated force & moment database might contain 6 output 

14force & moment coefficients that are a function of Mach number, angle 

15of attack, and sideslip angle. The file might only have data at Mach 

161.2 and 1.4, but it still has to provide an answer when the user wants 

17to know the forces & moments at Mach 1.32. The "response database" 

18contains some data along with explicitly specified rules. 

19 

20The :mod:`cape.attdb` module is intended to provide a library of tools 

21useful to this situation, with an obvious focus on the data products
